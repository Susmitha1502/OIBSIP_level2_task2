NAMBI NARAYANAN

Sir,

I know that we even lost the right to apologize but I really feel as guilty about what you received from us, though you did a lot for India.

You will always stay in our hearts NAMBI sir.

  • S. Nambi Narayanan is an Indian Aerospace engineer, who worked for the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and contributed significantly to Indian space program by developing the Vikas rocket engine.
  • S. Nambi Narayanan born on 12th December in 1941 at Nagercoil,Kanyakumari district,Tamil Nadu.
  • He completed his schooling at DVD Higher Secondary School,Nagercoil.
  • He did his Bachelor of Technology in Mechanical Engineering from Thiagarajar College of Engineering,Madurai.
  • After studying mechanical engineering in Madurai, Narayanan started his career in 1966 at ISRO as a technical assistant at the Thumba Equatorial Rocket Launching Station.
  • He was the first person to get expertise in Liquid propulsion at a time when Indian rocketry was still solely dependent on solid propellants
  • In 1974, Societe Europeenne de Propulsion agreed to transfer the Viking engine technology, Narayanan led the team of fifty-two engineers who worked on technology acquisition from French.
  • The first engine, named Vikas, was tested successfully in 1985.Nambi played a vital role in the innovation of VIKAS rocket engine.
  • He led the team which acquired technology from the French for the Vikas engine used in the first PSLV that India launched.
  • On 30 November 1994, Narayanan was arrested as part of an investigation of alleged espionage, by a team of Kerala police and Intelligence Bureau officials that he sold secrets to Pakistan.
  • In 1998,Supreme Court declared that Nambi Narayanan was exonerated.
  • He has worked with prominent scientists of India like Satish Dhawan,Vikram Sarabhai,Dr APJ Abdul Kalam,U.R.Rao etc, and has served ISRO for 35 years.
  • In 2001, the NHRC ordered the government of Kerala to pay him a compensation of ₹ 1 crore. He retired in 2001.
  • On 26 January 2019, he was conferred with the Padma Bhushan award, the third highest civilisation award, by the Government of India for developing the Vikas rocket engine.
  • In 2021, Kerala government settled the case filed against it by Narayanan by agreeing to a payment of ₹1.3 crore (US$160,000).
